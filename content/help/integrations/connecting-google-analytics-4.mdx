## Overview

**Google Analytics 4 (GA4)** integration lets you pull analytics data directly into Campaignly for audits, reporting, and campaign performance analysis. Once connected, you'll see key metrics without leaving the platform.

## What you can do

- View traffic, conversions, and user behavior data in Campaignly reports
- Run **analytics audits** to identify tracking issues or gaps
- Compare campaign performance across channels
- Export analytics data alongside your marketing metrics

## Requirements

Before connecting GA4, you'll need:

- Access to the GA4 property you want to connect
- **Editor** or **Administrator** role in that GA4 account (read-only access won't work)
- A Campaignly account with appropriate permissions

## Connect GA4 to Campaignly

### Step 1: Start the connection

1. In Campaignly, go to **Integrations** → **Analytics**.
2. Find **Google Analytics 4** and click **Connect**.
3. You'll be directed to Google's login page.

### Step 2: Authenticate with Google

1. Sign in with the Google account that has access to your GA4 property.
2. Review the permissions Campaignly is requesting (data read access only).
3. Click **Allow** to authorize the connection.

### Step 3: Select your GA4 property

1. You'll see a list of available GA4 properties.
2. Select the property you want to connect.
3. Click **Confirm**.

### Step 4: Verify the connection

You'll be returned to Campaignly. A green checkmark indicates the connection is active. You can now pull GA4 data into reports and audits.

## Using GA4 data in Campaignly

Once connected, you can:

- **Build reports** that include GA4 metrics (sessions, users, conversions, etc.)
- **Run analytics audits** to check tracking implementation
- **Add GA4 dimensions** to segment your campaign data

Data syncs automatically, though some reports may take a few minutes to populate.

## Disconnect or switch properties

Need to reconnect to a different GA4 property?

1. Go to **Integrations** → **Analytics**.
2. Find Google Analytics 4 and click **Manage**.
3. Select **Disconnect** or **Switch Property**.
4. Follow the authentication steps again.

## Troubleshooting

**Connection fails after clicking Allow**  
Check that your Google account has Editor or Admin access to the GA4 property. Read-only roles aren't supported.

**Data isn't appearing in reports**  
- Verify GA4 is actively tracking your site (check real-time data in GA4 itself).
- Allow 24–48 hours for historical data to sync fully.
- Re-authenticate if the connection is older than 6 months.

**"Property not found" error**  
Make sure you're selecting a GA4 property, not a Universal Analytics property. Campaignly only supports GA4.

## Need help?

If you run into issues, contact support with your Campaignly account email and the GA4 property ID (starts with "G-"). We'll investigate the connection from our end.
