Most campaign problems don't announce themselves. A client's Google Business Profile has the wrong phone number. Their landing page loads in six seconds on mobile. Their meta descriptions are missing on half their service pages. These aren't dramatic failures — they're quiet gaps that drain budget and suppress results. And when you're managing ten or fifteen clients, you don't have time to manually hunt for every one of them.

That's exactly what Campaignly's **campaign audit tool** was built for. It runs a structured check across the channels that matter most for local campaigns — search, ads, listings, and on-page SEO — and gives you a prioritized list of what needs fixing. This guide walks you through how to use it, how to read the results, and how to turn those results into action for your clients.

## What the Audit Tool Actually Checks

Campaignly's audit isn't a single-dimension scan. It evaluates multiple layers of a client's local marketing presence, then flags issues by severity. Here's what it covers:

**Google Business Profile (GBP)**
- NAP consistency (name, address, phone) across the profile and website
- Category selection and completeness
- Review count and average rating
- Photo quantity and recency
- Business description and attribute usage

**On-Page SEO**
- Title tags and meta descriptions (missing, duplicate, or too long)
- H1 structure
- Page speed on mobile and desktop
- Schema markup presence (especially LocalBusiness schema)
- Internal linking gaps

**Landing Pages**
- Load time
- Mobile responsiveness
- CTA clarity and placement
- Form functionality
- Tracking pixel or tag presence (Google Ads, Meta)

**Ad Campaign Alignment**
- Whether ad copy matches landing page messaging
- Keyword-to-landing-page relevance
- Conversion tracking status
- Geographic targeting accuracy

Each area gets scored individually, and you get a combined health score for the client. That score isn't meant to be a vanity metric — it's a triage tool.

## How to Run Your First Audit

Getting started takes about two minutes:

1. **Add your client's business** — Enter their website URL, business name, and primary location. If they have a Google Business Profile, connect it for deeper data.
2. **Select the audit scope** — You can run a full audit or limit it to specific areas (e.g., just SEO and GBP if you're not managing their ads).
3. **Hit "Run Audit"** — The tool crawls the site, checks external listings, and pulls in available ad account data. Most audits complete within 60–90 seconds.
4. **Review the results dashboard** — Issues appear grouped by category, color-coded by severity: red (critical), yellow (moderate), and blue (informational).

You don't need to connect every platform to get value. Even a URL-only audit will surface SEO and landing page problems that many clients don't know they have.

### A Quick Note on Permissions

If you want the ad alignment checks, you'll need read access to the client's Google Ads or Meta Ads account. Campaignly uses read-only connections — it never modifies campaigns or budgets.

## How to Interpret Your Audit Results

A long list of issues can feel overwhelming, especially when a client is watching. The key is understanding what the severity levels mean in practice.

| Severity | What It Means | Example |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Red — Critical** | Actively costing money or blocking conversions | Broken conversion tracking, wrong phone number on GBP, landing page returning a 404 |
| **Yellow — Moderate** | Reducing performance but not breaking anything | Missing meta descriptions, slow page speed, no LocalBusiness schema |
| **Blue — Informational** | Optimization opportunities, not urgent | Low photo count on GBP, thin business description, missing alt text on images |

**Start with red items.** These are the ones where a fix produces an immediate, measurable impact. A client whose Google Ads conversion tag is broken isn't just losing data — they're flying blind on what's working.

Yellow items are your next sprint. They tend to compound: a slow landing page with weak meta descriptions and no schema will consistently underperform, even if no single issue looks catastrophic on its own.

Blue items make great "proactive recommendations" in client reports. They show you're paying attention to details and give you a roadmap for ongoing optimization work.

### Don't Fix Everything at Once

This is a common trap. You see 23 issues and try to resolve all of them in a week. Instead, batch your fixes:

- **Week 1:** All red items
- **Week 2–3:** Yellow items, grouped by type (all SEO fixes together, all GBP fixes together)
- **Ongoing:** Blue items folded into your regular optimization cadence

This approach is easier to manage, easier to report on, and gives you clear before-and-after data for each round of fixes.

## Turning Audit Findings into Client Reports

Raw audit data is useful for you. It's not useful for most clients. They don't need to know what LocalBusiness schema is — they need to know that their business isn't showing up as well as it could in local search results.

Campaignly lets you export audit results into a **client-facing report** that translates technical findings into plain language. Each issue includes:

- A brief explanation of why it matters
- The estimated impact (high, medium, low)
- A recommended fix

You can customize which items appear in the report. This is important. If you're only hired to manage ads, sending a report full of SEO issues might confuse the scope — or it might be a great upsell opportunity. That's your call.

### Using Audits for Prospecting

Some agencies run audits *before* signing a client. A free audit gives a prospect a concrete look at what's wrong and positions you as the person who found it. It's specific, it's tangible, and it's a lot more persuasive than a generic capabilities deck.

You can run audits for any business with a public website — no account connection required for the baseline checks. Use the exportable report as your pitch document.

## How Often Should You Run Audits?

A one-time audit is a snapshot. Regular audits turn Campaignly into a monitoring system.

**Recommended cadence:**

- **Monthly** for active ad campaign clients (catch tracking issues, landing page changes, or GBP edits that someone made without telling you)
- **Quarterly** for SEO-only or GBP management clients
- **Before and after** any major change — site redesign, new landing pages, campaign restructure

Each audit is timestamped and stored, so you can compare results over time. This makes quarterly business reviews much easier: pull up the audit from three months ago, compare it to today, and show the client exactly what improved and what's left.
